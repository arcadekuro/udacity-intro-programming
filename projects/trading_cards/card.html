Koala

Koalas can only live in one place in the world, Australia.

  • Scientific Name: Phascolarctos cinereus
  • Average Length: 60 – 85 cm
  • Average Lifespan: 13 – 18 years
  • Habitat: Open eucalypt (gum tree) forests.

Koalas are well-known for their large, big furry ears and black nose. Koalas live in the eucalyptus forests of southeastern, eastern and north south wales of asutralia. Australia. They rely on the eucalyptus tree for both habitat and food. Sometimes mistaken for being 'high' koalas need to spend most of the day sleeping to digest their food.
